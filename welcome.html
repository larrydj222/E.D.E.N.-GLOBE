Mission

MAP E.D.E.N.

MAP E.D.E.N. is the operational field platform for Project E.D.E.N. (Earth Diametric & Energy Network)—a secure, repeatable way to run real-world tests along defined Earth corridors, beginning with the 42/222 diametric axis (42° and 222° are opposite bearings on the same straight line). Based on a 20+ year personal observation record, the Founder identified “222” as a persistent recurring numeric event marker across routine environments (time displays, identifiers, signage). This long-duration recurrence served as the initial trigger for structuring Project E.D.E.N. as a measurement-first field program.

Project E.D.E.N.

Over time, and in multiple documented sessions, the “222” event marker also coincided with a compass bearing of 222° (SW)—reinforcing the 42/222 line as a repeatable corridor for formal testing. MAP E.D.E.N. equips trusted operators with clear protocols to collect environmental field measurements and navigation-behavior observations, including human biological magnetoreception and magnetically influenced orientation, homing, and migratory navigation in animals and insects, so results can be compared across time and location.

Mandorla

Mandorla.io and Mandorla.app provide the logging and evidence backbone for Project E.D.E.N., enabling every MAP E.D.E.N. session to be executed as a standardized field test with consistent metadata and evidence requirements so events and signals are captured the same way every time and can be audited, replicated, and tracked over long baselines.

Mandorla (Parent Platform)

Mandorla (by Mandorla, Inc.) is a private technology and research company that operates a nonprofit research initiative dedicated to investigating repeating-number phenomena and pattern signals across terrestrial, oneiric (dream), and extraterrestrial/cosmic contexts. Through a peer-to-peer social-intelligence network, people around the world can log “222” and other repeating-number sightings, add real-time context, stories, and reflections, and see how their experiences connect to broader numerical, cosmological, and signal-detection models—providing the research foundation that powers Project E.D.E.N. via Mandorla.io and Mandorla.app.

Operator Access

Approved operators may receive access to MAP E.D.E.N. Operator Kits for field deployment. Operator Kits are distributed through secure channels and are available only via authorized download links provided within the MAP E.D.E.N. portal. Each kit contains the current protocols, checklists, and required logging standards to ensure tests are executed consistently, evidence is captured correctly, and results remain comparable across operators, locations, and time.

Boundary + Ethics

MAP E.D.E.N. is measurement-first. We prioritize evidence, controls, replication, and conservative interpretation—no claim is treated as established unless results repeat across independent tests. Privacy and safety come first: operator identities, exact private locations, and sensitive site details are protected, and fieldwork must follow all laws and respect wildlife, protected lands, and restricted areas.
